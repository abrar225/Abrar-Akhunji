The frontier of open-weight models just shifted dramatically. On July 16, 2026, Moonshot AI launched **Kimi K3**, a **2.8-trillion-parameter** multimodal behemoth engineered for long-horizon coding and end-to-end knowledge work. 

While the full weights are scheduled to drop on July 27, 2026, the model is already accessible via the Kimi API and web platform. The technical architecture behind this model introduces some serious structural innovations over its predecessor, K2, resulting in a **2.5× increase in scaling efficiency**.

### Architectural Breakthroughs
Kimi K3 manages its massive parameter count using a **Stable LatentMoE** framework. It consists of 896 total experts, activating just 16 per token to balance cutting-edge capability with efficient compute.

But the real magic lies in its attention mechanism:
1. **Kimi Delta Attention (KDA):** KDA is a hybrid linear attention mechanism that extends Gated DeltaNet. By incorporating a finer-grained, channel-wise gating mechanism, it drastically reduces reliance on traditional $O(T^2)$ softmax attention. The result? KV cache usage is slashed by up to 75%, and decoding throughput is significantly boosted.
2. **Attention Residuals (AttnRes):** Acting as a drop-in replacement for standard residual connections, AttnRes selectively retrieves representations across model depth. This provides smoother information flow across K3's deeper layers and longer sequences, delivering approximately 25% higher training efficiency.

These innovations allow Kimi K3 to flawlessly manage its **1-million-token context window** while natively supporting vision and "always-on" reasoning (thinking mode).

### Benchmark Dominance & Market Positioning
Kimi K3 was built to tackle complex, agentic workflows, and the benchmark data reflects this focus. As of mid-July 2026, it is ranked 4th on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, trailing only Claude Fable 5 and two configurations of GPT-5.6 Sol. 

Let's look at the numbers:
* **GPQA (Diamond):** 93.5% (The highest open-weight score published to date).
* **Terminal-Bench 2.1:** 88.3%
* **BrowseComp:** 91.2% (for long-context information retrieval).
